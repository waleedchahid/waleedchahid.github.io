Morrocan Couscous

Moroccan couscous is a dish that brings our family together on special occasions. It’s colorful, comforting, and full of spices that fill the house with warmth. We learned this recipe from my grandmother, who always said that the secret is in the slow steaming of the couscous and the rich vegetable broth.

"A family that eats couscous together stays together." -Mom

Tips & Tricks

  • Steam the couscous instead of boiling it for a fluffy texture.
  • Add a drizzle of olive oil between steamings to prevent clumping.
  • Use seasonal vegetables for the best flavor and color.

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot and sauté onions until golden.
  2. Add spices, and vegetables. Cover with water and simmer for 30–40 minutes.
  3. Steam the couscous over the broth for about 15 minutes, then fluff and steam again.
  4. Serve the couscous on a large plate with vegetables on top.
  5. Put remaining broth in a bowl with a spoon for people to add as much as they want to their side.

Prep time: 30 minutes
