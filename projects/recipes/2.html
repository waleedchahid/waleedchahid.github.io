Harira - Lentil and Chickpea Soup

Harira is a beloved Moroccan soup often served during Ramadan to brea fast. It’s hearty, full of protein, and tastes amazing. It has always been one of my favorite foods growing up, and still currently.

No Ramadan is complete without a pot of Harira. -mom

Tips & Tricks

  • Soak chickpeas overnight to make them cook faster and softer.
  • Use fresh herbs like cilantro and parsley for authentic flavor.
  • Add a squeeze of lemon before serving for brightness.

Recipe

IngredientAmount
Olive Oil2 tbsp
Onion (chopped)1 large
Celery (chopped)2 stalks
Lentils1 cup
Chickpeas (soaked)1 cup
Tomatoes (pureed)3
Tomatoes taste2-3 Tsp
Flour (for thickening)2 tbsp
Cilantro and Parsley (chopped)1/4 cup each
Turmeric, Ginger, Cinnamon1 tsp each
Salt and PepperTo taste
Water or Broth6 cups

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a pot and sauté onions and celery.
  2. Add spices, tomatoes, lentils, and chickpeas. Stir well.
  3. Pour in water or broth and simmer for about 1 hour.
  4. Mix flour with water and stir it into the soup to thicken slightly.
  5. Add fresh herbs and serve hot with lemon wedges.

Prep time: 20 minutes
Cook time: 1 hour
Servings: 6–8
